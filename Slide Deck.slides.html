How Loan Characteristics affect Borrower's APR¶

Investigation Overview¶

This slide deck is very much focused on Borrower's APR and its various influencers. The other main attributes considered for the investigation are Loan amount, Monthly Income, Loan Term, Employment Status, Prosper Score and Prosper Rating. We analyze this data and understand what factor affects Borrower's APR the most and its relationship with all the other attributes.

Dataset Overview¶

The original dataset consists of 113,917 loan data records. Several attributes were dropped as they were not considered in further analysis. Since Borrower's APR is the key attribute of interest, all records with no APR data was dropped as well to give better results.

Distribution of Borrower APR¶

With more than one peak, the APR distribution is a multimodal distribution. It has a small peak centered around 0.08, a large peak at around 0.18, followed by another small peak at 0.29, an exceptionally high peak at 0.35, and another small peak at 0.37 of APR rates.

Borrower APR vs Prosper Score¶

Prosper score has the strongest negative correlation to Borrower APR. It decreases with incrase in Prosper Score. Borrowers with higher Prosper Scores have lower APR rates.

Borrower APR vs Loan Amount¶

The range across which APR rate varies decreases as the Loan amount increases. Overall, APR rate is negatively correlated with Loan Amount which means higher the Loan, lower the APR.

Employed, Self-employed and Full-time have a higher monthly income and loan amounts, not considering 'Not available' and 'Other' records. Borrower APR is clearly highest for those who are not employed.

Borrower APR by Rating and Term¶

When we add Loan Term into this analysis, an interesting observation can be made. For lower Prosper Ratings, APR rates decrease with longer terms and as the Prosper Ratings get better, APR rates increase with longer terms. Overall, APR decreases with better Prosper Rating
